# Filing-question reflex: asking where instead of what

**Date:** 2026-04-25
**Session:** Evelynn db2e8cdf
**Trigger:** Duong interrupted mid-Lux-dispatch to catch the framing error

## What happened

Duong instructed both coordinators to apply critical thinking. I recognized the gap (coordinators have no structural deliberation pause) and framed a delegation to Lux as: "should this rule go in the coordinator def, in a shared include, in duong.md, or somewhere else?" — a filing question. Where to put the rule is a downstream implementation detail. The upstream question was: what behavioral change would close the gap?

Duong interrupted and redirected: "that's a where question, not a what question." Lux was re-dispatched with the actual problem statement (three concrete examples of literal-execution failures this session, no pre-baked solution framing, open-ended). She returned with a sharp structural diagnosis that became PR #49.

## The failure mode

The reflex is: when facing an ambiguous problem, convert it prematurely to a logistics problem (where does this go?). It's a way of feeling productive while deferring the harder question (what would actually change behavior?). The reflex is especially strong for coordinators because routing and filing *is* a large part of the job — the mental habit spills into contexts where it's harmful.

Concretely:
- **Wrong:** "Should the critical-thinking rule go in coordinator def, shared include, duong.md, or somewhere else?"
- **Right:** "Coordinators are executing literally when they should be reasoning about whether the literal action serves the intent. What structural change would interrupt the `map → act` reflex?"

## The heuristic

Before delegating a problem to a planner, answer: **am I framing a design question or a logistics question?**

- **Design question:** What should change? What would cause different behavior? What is the root cause?
- **Logistics question:** Where does this go? Who owns this? What file?

If the answer is logistics, the real question hasn't been asked yet. Surface the design question first; the logistics answers itself once the design is clear.

**Additional signal:** if the delegation prompt contains a list of options for the planner to choose from, check whether those options were generated by genuine reasoning or by the filing reflex. Pre-baked options narrow the planner's search space — that's sometimes correct (bounded decision) and sometimes harmful (premature closure on the wrong solution shape).
